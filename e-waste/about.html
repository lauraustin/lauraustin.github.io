This project investigates the concept of e-waste as a physical, digital, material, and processual phenomenon. It uses a broad definition of e-waste which includes obsolete electronics, appliances, and gadgets as well as dead apps and digital ephemera, such as old email invites, digital correspondences, and automated messages.

Key questions to consider include:
How do different communities think about waste creation or cycles?
What does it mean to “throw away” something? Where is “away?”
(How) can electronic waste be integrated effectively into waste remediation?
What kinds of digital ephemera do people retain? What do people discard?
What is something you wouldn’t hesitate to delete?
How is value derived in obsolete media?

Thank you to everyone who contributed their e-waste to this project. Look and listen carefully, perhaps you can find it!
